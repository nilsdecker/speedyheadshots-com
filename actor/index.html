Getting It Right

What casting directors
actually look for.

01

Match the mood to the role

A warm, friendly character calls for a light background and an open expression. A darker, more complex character needs lower contrast, moodier light. We'll help you think through the right approach for what you're submitting for.

02

Bring your range, not just a look

The best actor headshots aren't just attractive — they convey something. We spend time before the camera rolls understanding who you're playing and what you want a casting director to feel when they see your photo.

03

Wear the character, not an outfit

Dress as the character you want to be cast as. Your wardrobe should support the story you're telling, not distract from it. Bring multiple options and we'll advise on what's working with the light setup.

04

Natural retouching only

Casting directors need to recognise you when you walk in the room. Heavy retouching works against you. We remove minor blemishes and balance the light — nothing that changes what you actually look like.

05

Energy in the room matters

Getting the perfect shot is 99% the energy your photographer brings to the table. We're charged up, we direct, and we create an environment where emotions come naturally — not one where you're standing stiffly against a wall.

06

Fast turnaround for last-minute calls

Casting opportunities don't always give you two weeks' notice. We offer 24-hour standard delivery and same-day rush delivery on request. You book Tuesday, you have photos Wednesday. We've done it plenty of times.
