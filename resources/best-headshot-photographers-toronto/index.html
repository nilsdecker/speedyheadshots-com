What to look for in a Toronto headshot photographer

Before getting into specific studios, it's worth knowing what separates a good headshot photographer from a great one — because pricing alone won't tell you much.

  • Turnaround time: For most professional use cases, you need images within 24–48 hours. Some studios take 1–2 weeks.
  • Experience with your specific need: Actor headshots require a completely different skill set than corporate team photography. Don't hire an actor headshot specialist for a 30-person team shoot.
  • Live preview on set: The best studios tether their camera to a laptop so you can review shots in real time. This eliminates surprises at delivery.
  • Retouching approach: Natural retouching keeps you recognizable. Heavy skin smoothing looks great on screen and strange in person. Ask to see retouched examples.
  • What's actually included: "All digital images" is standard at good studios. If a photographer charges per image on top of the session fee, that's a red flag.

What to avoid

  • Photographers who don't show retouched examples. Anyone can take a sharp photo. The retouching is where the quality difference shows most clearly.
  • Studios that charge per digital image. All digitals should be included. Paying $25–50 per unretouched file on top of a session fee is a 2015 practice.
  • Unclear turnaround times. "As soon as possible" is not a delivery commitment. Get a specific timeframe before you book.
  • No live preview on set. If you can't see your shots during the session, you're booking blind.
